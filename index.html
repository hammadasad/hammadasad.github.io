Why CS?

Radio telescopes also looked cool to me and the idea of possibly working on one motivated me to enroll into the communications engineering program. My curriculumn differed from the electrical engineering one because we were exposed to more low-level software courses. After completing my second year, I decied to switch into computer science because I preferred the software aspect over the hardware. I did a lot of research before the switch and initially looked into going into the software engineering stream instead. I also did a summer internship right before where I was first exposed to real software development. All of this factored my decision to switch. So far, I can say that going into CS was one of the best decisions of my life.

Why make this?

To learn. A few of my friends had just compeleted 12-month work terms and I asked them of how they spent their off-work time. Most of them didn't get much accomplished because they were exhausted from work. They all mentioned that they wish they had spent more time learning new skills, coding and uncovering old hobbies. I decided that I want to be productive with my leisure time. Making this static webpage is a start and has allowed me to develop my HTML5 and CSS3 knowledge. Building this has also forced me to use the Bootstrap framework to allow me to space out columns and make the page look cleaner.
